Door locks

About:

Door locks provide essential security and privacy, acting as an important barrier to protect.Securing your home is a top priority, as it protects your valuable assets. To get the best security, make sure your doors are equipped with high-quality locks.

Padlocks

Padlocks are a widely recognized and preferred choice among door locks. These locks are not permanently installed; Instead, they offer portability and flexibility.

  • Available in a variety of sizes, padlocks are especially popular for securing storefronts, garages, two-way grill doors, and warehouses.
  • Shutter padlocks, in particular, stand out as a strong option for increasing security.

Deadbolts Lock

Deadbolts Lock are a top choice for securing main entrances to homes as they significantly reduce the risk of theft or burglary.

  • Additionally, they cannot be manipulated from the outside with tools or knives.
  • Among deadbolt types, single-cylinder deadbolts are the most straightforward, easily operated with a key from one side.

Doorknob locks

Doorknob locks represent another popular design that is mainly used for doors of interior rooms such as bathrooms and bedrooms.

  • For better security, it is recommended to install door handle locks with deadbolts on exterior doors.
  • Still, these types of apartment front door locks are convenient and user-friendly.
  • Unlike a deadbolt, in a door knob lock, the lock cylinder is located within the door knob itself.
  • However, it is important to note that these locks are not the most secure option, as they can be easily compromised with tools such as a hammer or heavy materials.

Mortise door locks

Mortise door locks are a highly preferred choice for large commercial doors, glass entry doors and expensive buildings.

  • In this configuration, rim cylinder locks are intricately installed inside the door, forming a long metal piece that extends outward.Mortise locks are renowned for their strong security, making them an ideal choice for commercial entry doors.
  • Mortise locks come in a variety of configurations, including three-lever and five-lever models.
  • The greater the number of levers, the higher level of security these locks provide.

Electronic locks

Electronic locks represent a modern and keyless locking solution. Instead of traditional keys, they rely on a card or keypad with a unique code to provide access when interfaced with the lock.

  • These locks are commonly used in office settings and hotels, providing convenience and security.
  • They function using electrical power or batteries, ensuring longevity.
  • Recent advances in electronic locks have also allowed remote entry authorization, eliminating the need for physical cards.
